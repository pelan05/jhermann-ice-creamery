# Roasted Sweet Potato [1.4l]

> From the transcript of [SORGHUM Roasted SWEET Potato Ice Cream Recipe](https://youtu.be/6v20E0i0X00).

**Composition of the base**

| Fat % | Sugar % | Total Solids % | Overrun |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ≈ 16% | ≈ 21% | ≈ 44% | ≈ 25% |

This recipe is a fun twist on an "earthy" dessert! Roasting the sweet potato with sorghum syrup creates a deep, caramelized flavor that tastes like a sweet potato pie in ice cream form.

We use a special trick here: curdling the heavy cream with apple cider vinegar. Don't worry — it doesn't taste like vinegar! It just creates a super thick and silky texture without needing to cook an egg custard.

If you can't find sorghum syrup, maple syrup or honey work perfectly, too.

## Ingredients

**Roasted Potato Purée**

*   300g [Sweet Potato (Orange Flesh)](https://afcd.foodstandards.gov.au/fooddetails.aspx?PFKID=F010942) (≈ 1 large potato, chopped)
*   60g [Sorghum Syrup (Proxy: Golden Syrup)](https://afcd.foodstandards.gov.au/fooddetails.aspx?PFKID=F009049) (≈ 3 tbsp)
*   80g [Full Cream Milk](https://afcd.foodstandards.gov.au/fooddetails.aspx?PFKID=F005634) (≈ 1/3 cup, for blending)

*Subtotal Weight*: 440g

**Ice Cream Base**

*   515g [Heavy Cream 35% Fat](https://afcd.foodstandards.gov.au/fooddetails.aspx?PFKID=F003270) (≈ 2 cups)
*   30g [Apple Cider Vinegar (Proxy: Vinegar)](https://afcd.foodstandards.gov.au/fooddetails.aspx?PFKID=F009498) (≈ 2 tbsp)
*   400g [Sweetened Condensed Milk](https://afcd.foodstandards.gov.au/fooddetails.aspx?PFKID=F005582) (≈ 1 standard can)

*Subtotal Weight*: 945g

## Directions

1.  **Base Prep (Roasting)**: Chop the sweet potato into small cubes and toss them with the sorghum syrup until fully coated. Spread them on a baking tray with parchment paper and roast in a hot oven (≈ 200°C / 400°F) until they are caramelized and soft.
2.  **Base Prep (Puréeing)**: Scrape the sticky roasted potatoes into a blender. Add the milk and blend until completely smooth and liquid.
3.  **Base Prep (Curdling)**: In a large bowl, whisk the heavy cream with the apple cider vinegar. Put it in the fridge for a few minutes to let the acid thicken the cream.
4.  **Mixing**: Whisk the potato purée into the thickened cream until the color is a beautiful, even orange. Scrape in the sweetened condensed milk and whisk until everything is well combined.
5.  **Churning**: Pour the mixture into your ice cream machine. Churn until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.
6.  **Hardening**: Move the ice cream to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours (or overnight) until firm.
